Welcome to the Iris Flower Classifier

Classify iris flower species using sepal and petal features with the power of machine learning.

About Us

This web application is designed to accurately classify iris flower species using a machine learning model trained on the widely recognized Iris dataset. The dataset includes measurements of petal and sepal length and width—key features that help differentiate between the three main species of iris: Setosa, Versicolor, and Virginica.

Our goal is to demonstrate how machine learning can be applied in a practical and intuitive way, especially for those just beginning their journey into the world of AI. With a clean interface, this project bridges the gap between complex algorithms and real-world applications by offering a smooth user experience and insightful results.

Whether you're a student, researcher, or simply curious about data science, this classifier serves as a great learning tool to understand the fundamentals of classification models and how feature-based predictions work.

About the Iris Flower

What is a Sepal and Petal?

- Sepals are the green leaf-like structures that protect the flower bud before it opens. They are typically broader and shorter than petals.

- Petals are the colorful parts of the flower that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. They are usually more delicate and are typically larger than the sepals.

The dimensions of the sepal (length and width) and petal (length and width) are key features used to classify the Iris species. By measuring these dimensions, machine learning models can differentiate between species based on the unique patterns of sepal and petal sizes.





Iris Setosa

Iris setosa is known for its shorter petals and sepals. It has broad, dark green leaves and small, round flowers.

Iris Versicolor

Iris versicolor has medium-sized flowers with distinct blue-violet petals and a white stripe running through the middle. Its sepal and petal sizes fall between those of setosa and virginica.

Iris Virginica

Iris virginica has large petals with deep purple-blue colors and a prominent yellow marking at the base of the petals. It has larger sepal and petal dimensions compared to the other two species.
